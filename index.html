Step by Step

Project Development Lifecycle

From initial registration through construction to final handover to the Government of Nepal.

1

Company Registration

Register at CRO, DOI, and Tax Office. FDI approval needed for foreign participation.

2

Survey License

Issued via DoED/MoE; valid for max 5 years for feasibility studies.

3

Feasibility & Environment

IEE for projects <50MW; EIA for >50MW. Approved by MoE/MoFE.

4

PPA Execution

Agreement with NEA. Requires technical feasibility and bank letter of intent.

5

Additional Approvals

Forestry, National Parks, and Road Department clearances as applicable.

6

Financial Closure

Submission of cost data and debt-equity ratio (usually 70:30).

7

Generation License

Issued for 30–35 years (max 50). Marks the start of construction.

8

Construction

Implementation phase following DoED standards and O&M safety rules.

9

Operation

Power generation and royalty management via the DoED system.

10

Handover

Assets revert to GON for free after license expiry (Right of Reversion).

Important Notes & Conditions

  1. If dry season energy is less than 35% of annual energy, a storage project shall be considered as a PROR project for applying the power purchase rate.
  2. Flat power purchase rate shall be applicable for multipurpose storage projects (Dry % × 12.40 + Wet % × 7.10).
  3. Active storage volume should not be less than 15 days design discharge. Dead storage must resist sediment fill for at least 50 years.
  4. A PROR project must provide daily peaking power at rated capacity for minimum 1 to 6 hours at one time.
  5. Power purchase rate for PROR dry season peaking energy shall be as per actual, approved once a year by the system operator after commissioning.
  6. If dry season energy falls below 30% after commissioning despite PPA commitment, excess annual energy above the 30%-based estimate shall be adjusted as compensation in the next year's bill.
  7. New rates and rules apply only to projects concluded from Baisakh 14, 2074 onwards.
  8. For foreign currency denominated PPA, NEA Board has approved guidelines based on the above rates and terms.

* If wet season energy > 50%, rate decreases by the excess %. If dry energy < 35%, project is treated as PROR.
