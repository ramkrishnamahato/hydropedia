Project Development Lifecycle

1

Company Registration

Register at CRO, DOI, and Tax Office. FDI approval needed if foreign.

2

Survey License

Issued via DoED/MoE. Valid for max 5 years to conduct feasibility studies.

3

Feasibility & Enviro

IEE for projects <50MW; EIA for >50MW. Approving Authority: MoE/MoFE.

4

PPA Execution

Power Purchase Agreement with NEA. Requires feasibility and LOI from banks.

5

Additional Approvals

Forestry, National Parks, and Road Dept clearances as applicable.

6

Financial Closure

Submission of PPA, project cost, and debt-equity ratio (usually 70:30).

7

Generation License

Issued for 30-35 years (Max 50). Allows start of physical construction.

8

Construction

Implementation phase following DoED standards and O&M safety rules.

9

Operation

Power generation and royalty management via DoED system .

10

Handover

Transfer of assets to GON for free after license expiry (Right of Reversion).

Forest & Land Acquisition

If the project area includes national forests, developers must:

  • Obtain lease approval from the Ministry of Forest and Soil Conservation (MFSC).
  • Plant 10 trees for every 1 tree cut (1:10 ratio) and maintain them for 5 years.
  • Plant trees equal to the project area if using national forest land.
